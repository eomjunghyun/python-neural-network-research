# Research Handoff - 2026-04-09

This file records the current research status for the sinusoid-representation project so work can continue cleanly on another machine.

## 1. Current research direction

The main paper protocol now treats the problem as a discrete-time signal modeling problem:

\[
y_n = \sum_{j=1}^{k} a_j \sin(\theta_j n + \phi_j)
\]

with:

- `time_mode="discrete"` for the main experiments
- theory dimension defined as `2k`
- numerical rank used only as a diagnostic
- alignment metrics based on sampled basis matrix `F` versus hidden feature matrix `H`
- main comparison done on the subspaces spanned by `F` and `H`

Continuous-time support still exists in the codebase, but the current paper experiments are centered on the discrete-time setup.

## 4. Completed experiments

### Experiment 1 - metric sanity

Purpose:

- verify that the new alignment metrics behave correctly on synthetic hand-constructed `H`

Status:

- completed successfully

Observed result:

- `same_subspace` gives essentially perfect results
- `random` gives very low overlap
- `random_perp` gives effectively zero overlap and `90 deg` angles
- `same_plus_orth_noise` gives full coverage but reduced purity, which is the intended behavior

Interpretation:

- the metric family distinguishes correct subspace structure from unrelated or orthogonal structure
- full-space and top-diagnostic metrics behave differently in meaningful ways

### Experiment 2 - linear recovery

Purpose:

- check that a linear model recovers the correct structure under relatively easy conditions

Status:

- completed successfully

Observed result summary:

- across the tested `k` and `L` combinations, the linear model achieved:
  - very low test MSE
  - `test_align_coverage_full_mean ~= 1.0`
  - `test_recon_r2_qf_from_h_mean ~= 1.0`
  - `test_align_mean_angle_deg_full_mean ~= 0`

Interpretation:

- the linear baseline essentially recovers the true sampled basis subspace in easy discrete-time conditions
- prediction error worsens somewhat as `k` grows and lag becomes relatively smaller, but subspace recovery remains extremely strong

## 6. Meaning of the main alignment metrics

The most important alignment metrics are:

- `align_coverage_full`
  - how much of the theoretical basis space is covered by the hidden-feature space
- `align_purity_full`
  - how much of the hidden-feature space lies inside the theoretical basis space
- `align_mean_angle_deg_full`
  - average principal angle between the hidden space and the theoretical basis space
- `recon_r2_qf_from_h`
  - how well the orthonormal basis of the theoretical space can be linearly reconstructed from `H`

Important conceptual point:

- the comparison is **not** column-to-column matching
- the comparison is between:
  - `span(F)` and `span(H)`

So the project compares the subspace generated by the true sampled sin/cos basis and the subspace generated by the network's hidden features.

## 7. Current interpretation of the project

The project is now clearly focused on:

- learning next-step prediction on discrete-time sums of sinusoids
- studying whether the hidden representation recovers the true `2k`-dimensional sinusoidal subspace
- separating:
  - prediction quality
  - subspace recovery quality
  - numerical conditioning / diagnostic issues

Current evidence from experiments 1-4:

- the metric framework behaves sensibly
- the linear baseline strongly recovers the correct space
- nonlinear models can predict well without matching the theoretical space as perfectly
